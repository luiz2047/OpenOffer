# OpenOffer AGPL FAQ

OpenOffer is licensed under the GNU Affero General Public License v3.0.

This is a plain-language summary, not legal advice. The license text in [LICENSE](../LICENSE) controls.

## Why AGPL?

OpenOffer handles sensitive local workflows: resumes, recruiter chats, transcripts, meeting notes, provider credentials, and interview context. AGPL keeps improvements to the shared product visible when someone modifies the software and offers it to others over a network.

## Can I use OpenOffer personally?

Yes. You can run it locally, inspect it, modify it, and use your own provider keys.

## Can my company use it internally?

Generally yes, subject to AGPL obligations and your company's policies. If you distribute modified copies or provide a modified network service based on OpenOffer, you need to understand and follow the AGPL.

## Can I fork it?

Yes. Forks are welcome. Keep the license and notices intact, and publish source for modified versions when the AGPL requires it.

## Can I build a hosted service from it?

The AGPL has network-use obligations. If you modify OpenOffer and let users interact with it over a network, you should expect to provide corresponding source code for your modified version.

## Can I connect commercial AI providers?

Yes. The OpenOffer license does not prevent you from using paid third-party APIs. Their own terms and privacy policies still apply.

## Does AGPL force my private resumes, transcripts, or notes to be public?

No. The license applies to the software and modified source code, not to your private user data.

## I want a different license for a commercial integration. What should I do?

Open an issue or discussion describing the integration and why AGPL does not fit. Do not assume dual licensing exists unless maintainers explicitly provide it.
